<h2><strong><u>What to Wear for Women</u></strong></h2><p class="ql-align-justify">	Women should wear a combination of a skirt or dress slacks, blouse, sweater, twinset, jacket (optional), and hosiery (optional) with closed toe shoes. Sandals or peep-toe shoes may be permissible in some offices but save flip-flops for the weekend. Any working girl should have the following staples in her wardrobe:</p><p class="ql-align-justify"><br></p><ul><li class="ql-align-justify"><span class="ql-font-serif">Khaki, corduroy, twill, or cotton pants or conservative-length skirts</span></li><li><span class="ql-font-serif">Sweaters, twinsets, cardigans, polo/knit shirts</span></li><li><span class="ql-font-serif">A professional dress - try a sheath silhouette</span></li><li><span class="ql-font-serif">Solid colors are preferable over bright patterns</span></li></ul><p><br></p><h2><strong><u>What to Wear for Men</u></strong></h2><p>	For men, appropriate business casual attire is dress slacks or chinos, a button-down shirt, dark socks, and dress shoes. Avoid wearing polo shirts to an interview, even if they are acceptable for the job in question. Do not wear jeans or shorts. The following will help you solidify good standing at a new position:</p><p><br></p><ul><li><span class="ql-font-serif">Khaki, gabardine, wool, or cotton pants, neatly pressed</span></li><li><span class="ql-font-serif">Cotton long-sleeve, button-down shirts, pressed</span></li><li><span class="ql-font-serif">Sweaters</span></li><li><span class="ql-font-serif">Leather shoes, in black and brown</span></li><li><span class="ql-font-serif">Leather belt, in black and brown</span></li><li><span class="ql-font-serif">A selection of ties</span></li></ul>
